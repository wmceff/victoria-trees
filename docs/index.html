The Trees of Victoria

About Victoria Trees

This app helps you identify and learn a bit about the many diverse trees in the City of Victoria.

Victoria stands on the traditional territories of the lək̓ʷəŋən peoples, the Songhees and Esquimalt Nations. Where known, SENĆOŦEN tree names are provided, with a link to a website where you can hear the pronounciation. SENĆOŦEN is the language of the W̱SÁNEĆ People, and a member of the North Straits Salish dialect continuum along with lək̓ʷəŋən - the names would be pronounced similarly in lək̓ʷəŋən.

The tree data is mainly from the City of Victoria's trees dataset - we've done some cleanup and made some additions. If your tree is missing, it could be because it's not on public property, or that the city isn't keeping track of it. Check out iNaturalist or PictureThis to help with identification.

Inspired by James Clowater's website Trees of Victoria. We've cleaned up the City's data and provided some additional points of interest about the trees.
